Butter Chicken Curry

"Indulge in the ultimate comfort food with our rich, creamy butter chicken curry served alongside fragrant rice and warm, pillowy naan bread."

Butter chicken, or "Murgh Makhani," is a classic North Indian dish that originated in Delhi during the 1950s. It was born out of resourcefulness when leftover tandoori chicken was mixed with a buttery tomato sauce, accidentally creating the rich, creamy curry we know and love today. This dish shows that with a bit of creativity, even leftovers can turn into something truly wonderful. Butter chicken is wholesome, packed with flavor, and leaves you craving more with every bite—exactly why I chose to share this recipe!

How to make it:

Preparation Time: 15 Minutes

Cook Time: 45 Minutes

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:
  • (500g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs (cut into bite-size pieces)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • Salt to taste
For the Butter Sauce:
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • (400g) tomato puree
  • 1 cup fresh cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp sugar or honey (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
For the Basmati Rice:
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
For the Naan Bread:
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Melted butter for brushing

Method:

  1. Marinate the Chicken

    In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, lemon juice, garam masala, turmeric, cumin, chili powder, garlic, ginger, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and coat them well. Cover and marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for deeper flavor).

  2. Cook the Rice

    Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. In a pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil, add salt, and the rice. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Fluff with a fork and keep warm.

  3. Make the Naan Dough

    In a large bowl, combine flour, baking powder, baking soda, yogurt, olive oil, salt, and warm water. Mix until a dough forms. Knead for 5-7 minutes until smooth. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

  4. Cook the Chicken

    In a pan, heat 1 tbsp butter over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken and cook until browned and cooked through (about 8-10 minutes). Remove and set aside.

  5. Prepare the Butter Chicken Sauce

    In the same pan, melt the remaining butter. Add the onions and sauté until golden and soft. Add garlic, ginger, garam masala, cumin, paprika, and coriander. Cook until fragrant (about 2 minutes). Stir in the tomato puree and cook for 5 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.

    Stir in the cream, and add salt, pepper, and sugar/honey to balance the flavors. Simmer for 10 minutes. Add the cooked chicken back to the sauce and simmer for another 5 minutes.

  6. Cook the Naan

    Divide the dough into 6 balls. Roll each ball into an oval shape (about 1/4 inch thick). Heat a non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Cook each naan for 2-3 minutes on one side, then flip and cook the other side until puffed and golden brown. Brush with melted butter.

Final Assembly:

Serve the butter chicken hot, garnished with fresh coriander leaves. Pair it with a side of fluffy basmati rice and warm, buttery naan bread for a truly comforting and flavorful feast.
